What I Study

I like taking abstract concepts and whittling them down to something simple and precise. Analysis is where I learned to do that rigorously, while proofs are where I get to be creative. Quantum information theory is where the math builds something applicable in the real world: new algorithms and new ways to encode and transmit information. That pipeline from abstraction to algorithm is what I find most interesting about math.

More About Me

I've loved games since I was a kid. I grew up playing chess and League of Legends. Chess taught me to think three moves ahead. League taught me that systems have patterns you can learn to exploit. That same mindset followed me into math, into AI, and eventually into a poker bot competition at Princeton where I finished 2nd place. Games have always been the most interesting motivation of my life.
